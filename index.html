About Me

I’m a quantitative researcher focused on turning noisy market data into measurable signals, risk-aware portfolios, and deployable research workflows. My work emphasizes leakage-safe validation, interpretable diagnostics, and disciplined exposure control — so results remain credible out-of-sample and actionable under constraints.

I’m especially interested in volatility modeling and regime behavior (EGARCH / regime switching), factor attribution (IC testing, Fama–MacBeth), and portfolio optimization under exposure constraints (risk parity, multi-factor control). I also enjoy bridging model logic with “real-world tradability”: transaction costs, execution quality, and automated reporting.
